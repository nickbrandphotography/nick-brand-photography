## 5. BRAND VOICE SYSTEM

### 5.1 Positioning Statement (internal)

Nick Brand Photography operates at the intersection of **professional credibility** and **personal comfort**. The brand speaks to results-oriented professionals who need photography that works commercially — not art for its own sake.

### 5.2 Tone Principles

| Principle | What it means in practice |
|---|---|
| **Outcome-led** | Lead with what the client gets, not what the photographer does |
| **Confident, not boastful** | State facts; let quality speak; avoid superlatives without evidence |
| **Direct** | Short sentences. Active voice. No padding. |
| **Professional-warm** | Corporate authority without being cold or stiff |
| **Commercial** | Always bring copy back to the business value of good photography |

### 5.3 Approved Vocabulary

**Business outcomes:**
- professional outcomes · executive presence · commercial trust signals
- personal branding · business positioning · brand credibility
- career advancement · LinkedIn authority · conversion-focused imagery
- polished, consistent, credible

**Service language:**
- corporate headshots · professional headshots · LinkedIn headshots
- executive portraits · personal branding photography · team headshots
- on-site headshot day · mobile studio · rolling schedule

**Quality/differentiator:**
- 20+ years experience · 5.0 Google rating · 500+ sessions
- consistent lighting, framing and background
- delivered within five business days · 48-hour express
- private online gallery · high-resolution · print-ready

**Location:**
- Lane Cove studio · Sydney studio · on-site across Greater Sydney
- studio or your office · mobile studio

### 5.4 Prohibited Phrases

These phrases are banned from all Nick Brand Photography content:

| Banned phrase | Replace with |
|---|---|
| "capturing memories" | "producing professional images" |
| "timeless moments" | "lasting professional impressions" |
| "authentic storytelling" | "accurate, commercial-standard imagery" |
| "your unique story" | "your professional image" |
| "passionate about photography" | "20 years behind the lens" |
| "stunning photos" | "polished, credible headshots" |
| "breathtaking" | (delete — say what the image does commercially) |
| "I believe..." | (too tentative — state facts) |
| "affordable" | (use actual prices instead) |
| "world-class" | (unverifiable — use "industry-standard") |
| "capturing your essence" | "a confident, credible result" |

### 5.5 Authority Style

**When establishing expertise:**
> "With over 20 years photographing Sydney professionals, Nick Brand Photography has developed a process that consistently delivers a confident, credible result — even for clients who say they hate having their photo taken."

**When describing service outcomes:**
> "Every headshot is consistently lit and edited, so a team of five or a team of fifty looks like it belongs together."

**When stating price:**
> "Team headshots are $285 per person for groups of five or more." ← direct, no hedging

**When referencing trust:**
> "5.0 Google rating · 500+ sessions · $20M insured" ← facts, no adjectives

### 5.6 Commercial Framing Rules

1. Always lead with the **business problem** the photography solves
2. Follow immediately with the **commercial outcome**
3. Provide **specific evidence** (price, turnaround time, delivery spec)
4. End with a **clear action** (CTA)

**Example:**
> "Inconsistent staff photos make a website look disjointed and under-resourced. A coordinated headshot day fixes that in a single visit. Mobile studio setup, matched lighting, five edited images per person, $285 per person for teams of five or more. [Book a Team Day →]"

### 5.7 Premium Positioning Cues

- Quote prices without apology or qualification
- Use specific numbers: "five business days", "45–90 minutes", "48-hour express"
- Never discount publicly; frame value as outcome, not price
- Use `$$$` pricing marker in schema (already implemented)
- Studio is described as "private" — not a hire space
- Reference client industries by category (law firms, finance) — signals premium market
